# Hack This Hack: Building Your Own Uchronies

> This section separates the mechanics from the setting. Las Vegas 2080 is one implementation of the system. This is the system itself.

## What Makes Uchronies Work

The game runs on three interlocking pressures:

1. **A structural deficit**: the characters cannot survive on legitimate income alone. This forces real moral choices rather than optional ones.
2. **A slow corroding force**: something erodes the characters over time, creating a natural campaign timer and making every mission feel costly.
3. **A competing power structure**: a second faction that offers shortcuts with escalating costs, positioned to fill the gap left by the structural deficit.

Every other element (the Division, Zhou, Las Vegas, the temporal zones) is an implementation of these three pressures. You can replace all of them.

## The Five Variables to Redefine

To move the system to any other setting, redefine these five elements. Everything else can remain unchanged.

| Variable | In Las Vegas 2080 | What to Redefine |
|---|---|---|
| **The employer** | The Division / Agent Hayes | Who pays the characters, in what currency, with what hierarchy and what power to punish |
| **The corruptor** | Madame Zhou / certificates | Who offers the shortcut, what their currency is, what they want in return, at what cost |
| **The corrosive force** | Temporal exposure / WIL damage | What force degrades the characters over time, which attribute it erodes, how it manifests visibly |
| **The deficit** | -$100/week at Recruit tier | Which resource is structurally insufficient and why legitimate income cannot cover it |
| **The echo** | Temporal echo (hostile NPC) | What a character becomes when corruption or corrosion fully consumes them |

## What Does NOT Need to Change

The following mechanics are setting-agnostic and transfer cleanly to any context:

- The save system (d20 under attribute, advantage/disadvantage)
- The Cairn combat engine (auto-hit, damage dice, HP as buffer)
- The Quirk structure (permanent effects with benefits and social costs, limit of 5)
- The Loyalty/Corruption dual-tracker with intermediate thresholds
- The inventory system (10 slots, bulky items, full inventory = 0 HP)
- The contamination mechanic (periodic saves, WIL damage, cap at half current WIL)
- The tier progression (3 tiers, pay increases, no mechanical bonuses)

## Three Transposition Examples

### Example 1: Cosmic Horror, 1920s

**The employer.** A secret society of occult scholars. Pay is a stipend that covers rent in a boarding house but not equipment or travel. The society is bureaucratic, underfunded, and does not reimburse field expenses.

**The corruptor.** A cult that trades in Forbidden Knowledge (a separate currency, like certificates). The cult does not want loyalty. It wants specific books, artifacts, and the occasional witness silenced.

**The corrosive force.** Exposure to cosmic entities erodes WIL. Quirks become obsessions, physical marks, and perceptual distortions. The echo equivalent is a cultist who has been fully possessed: the original personality is gone, a fragment remains.

**The deficit.** Expeditions cost more than the society reimburses. Equipment, travel, bribes, and local contacts come out of pocket. The society covers nothing retroactively.

**The echo.** A possessed investigator, controlled by the Warden. Retains fragments of the original character's memories. May speak in their voice. Is not them.

---

### Example 2: Corporate Sci-Fi

**The employer.** A megacorporation. The characters are troubleshooters or field operatives. Pay is competitive but corporate deductions (equipment loans, housing, insurance premiums, mandatory augmentation maintenance) create a perpetual deficit.

**The corruptor.** A competitor corporation that pays in untraceable credits. Every transaction is a liability: the competitor wants intel, sabotage, and eventually personnel.

**The corrosive force.** Malfunctioning implants degrade DEX or STR. Quirks become hardware glitches, phantom inputs, and loyalty-module interference. The echo equivalent is a fully reprogrammed operative: identity wiped, running on corporate firmware.

**The deficit.** Augmentation maintenance is mandatory and expensive. Missing payments means the implants degrade faster, creating a feedback loop between the financial pressure and the corrosive force.

**The echo.** A reprogrammed agent with no accessible memories. Mechanically identical to a temporal echo. The tragedy is different.

---

### Example 3: Dark Fantasy

**The employer.** A hunters' guild or garrison command. Pay covers basic lodging and rations, but not equipment, healing, or anti-corruption treatments. The guild takes a percentage of artifact recovery.

**The corruptor.** A criminal lord or chthonic cult. Pays in unmarked coin. Wants specific artifacts, corpses, and silence. Every transaction costs +1 Corruption.

**The corrosive force.** Magical corruption from cursed zones erodes WIL. Quirks manifest as physical marks, prophetic fragments, and compulsions. The echo equivalent is a thrall or possessed fighter: the body still moves, the person is gone.

**The deficit.** Anti-corruption treatments (the fantasy equivalent of anti-rejection medicine) cost more than weekly guild pay. A character who skips treatment becomes Deprived and cannot recover WIL.

**The echo.** A thrall or possessed creature under the corruptor's control. Retains muscle memory. Fights with the original character's weapons. Recognizes former companions.

## The Minimum Viable Uchronies

If you want to strip the setting entirely and run a one-shot or con game in a new context, you need:

1. A one-paragraph description of the employer and their pay structure, including the deficit.
2. A name and one-line description of the corruptor and what they offer.
3. A name for the corrosive force and which attribute it damages.
4. Three Quirks appropriate to the setting (you do not need all 12 for a one-shot).
5. One moral dilemma prepared in advance (two options, both costly, both defensible).

Everything else, the save system, the inventory, the combat rules, the tier progression, transfers without modification.
