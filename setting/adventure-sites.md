## The Crimson Palace (Zhou's Headquarters)

**Zone:** Yellow. **Contamination:** WIL save every 8 hours, d4.
**Why PCs go here:** Buy from Zhou, sell to Zhou, infiltrate for Hayes, or attend an "invitation."

### Map

```
[STREET] ─── [A. GATE] ─── [B. COURTYARD]
                                  │
                    ┌─────────────┼─────────────┐
                    │             │             │
              [C. MARKET]   [D. LOUNGE]   [E. FIGHTS]
                    │             │
              [F. VAULT]   [G. ZHOU'S OFFICE]
                                  │
                          [H. PRIVATE ROOMS]
```

### Rooms

**A. The Gate.** Two mercenaries (6 HP, 1 Armor, 12 STR, 10 DEX, 8 WIL, SMG d6 blast) check visitors. No weapons allowed inside. They confiscate and tag everything. Getting weapons back requires Zhou's approval or a DEX save to palm something small.

**B. The Courtyard.** Open-air atrium of a former luxury hotel. Neon signs from three different decades flicker simultaneously: a 1950s cocktail lounge sign, a 2020s holographic billboard, a 2080s digital ticker showing black market prices. 20-30 people milling around. Reaction roll for anyone you approach.

**C. The Market.** Three stalls run by Zhou's people. **Guns** (all weapons at 150% standard price, no questions). **Medicine** (anti-rejection at $200 instead of $250, +1 Corruption per purchase). **Information** (rumors about Division operations, $100-$500, WIL save to determine if the info is real or planted). If PCs buy medicine here regularly, Zhou notices and uses it as leverage later.

**D. The Lounge.** Red velvet, dim amber lighting, jazz from 1942 playing on a temporal loop. This is where Zhou meets new "clients." Tommy "Dice" Martinez works the bar. He'll sell info about anyone in the room for $200. Three tables of gamblers. One table is rigged (DEX save to notice the dealer's sleight).

**Dilemma at the table:** A Division agent you recognize is here, clearly making a deal. Do you report them to Hayes (+1 Loyalty, but the agent will know it was you) or ignore it (no consequence now, but you have leverage for later)?

**E. The Fighting Pit.** Underground cage fights. Spectators bet. PCs can fight for $200-$500 per match. Opponent: 6 HP, 14 STR, 10 DEX, 8 WIL, fists d4 (enhanced if they grapple). If PCs win 3 fights, Zhou invites them upstairs. If they lose, they owe Zhou for medical costs.

**F. The Vault.** Behind the market, locked door (DEX save to pick, or bribe the guard $500). Contains Zhou's artifact collection: 2d6 temporal artifacts worth $1,000-$10,000 each. Stealing from Zhou is possible but she will find out within 48 hours. Consequences: mercenaries sent, or worse, Zhou cuts off medicine supply.

**G. Zhou's Office.** Top floor suite. Elegant: mahogany desk, calligraphy scrolls, a single orchid. Zhou sits behind the desk. She already knows why you're here. She always knows. Two bodyguards (8 HP, 2 Armor, 14 STR, 12 DEX, 10 WIL, assault rifle d8) stand behind her. Zhou never threatens directly. She offers. She waits. She smiles.

**H. Private Rooms.** Where Zhou conducts business she doesn't want overheard. Sound-dampened. No windows. If you're invited here, the offer is big and the price is permanent. This is where agents sign away their loyalty.

## Division HQ (The Aria)

**Zone:** Green. **Contamination:** None.
**Why PCs go here:** Briefings, medical treatment, equipment requisition, internal politics.

### Map

```
[STREET] ─── [A. CHECKPOINT] ─── [B. LOBBY]
                                       │
                         ┌─────────────┼──────────────┐
                         │             │              │
                   [C. ARMORY]   [D. BRIEFING]   [E. INFIRMARY]
                                       │
                                 [F. OFFICES]
                                       │
                              [G. HAYES' OFFICE]
                                       │
                           [H. BASEMENT (RESTRICTED)]
```

### Rooms

**A. Checkpoint.** Metal detectors, badge scanners, two guards. Standard procedure. If a PC has Corruption 4+, guards give them a longer look. At Corruption 7+, they're pulled aside for a "random" inspection (WIL save or they find something compromising).

**B. Lobby.** Sterile, corporate. A wall of photos: agents killed in action. 47 faces. PCs will recognize some from briefings. Notice board: mission postings, Benson's announcements ("Form 16-C deadline extended to Friday"), a handwritten note ("If anyone finds my left shoe in the Yellow Zone, it's a size 10. Thanks. - Rodriguez").

**C. Armory.** Sergeant Willis. Standard equipment issued for free. Anything beyond standard requires Form 16-C (see Benson) or Loyalty 7+. Contains: standard firearms, tactical vests, temporal protection suits, flashlights, rations, radios. Does NOT contain: assault weapons, heavy armor, explosives. Those are "restricted to Priority Alpha missions" (meaning: buy from Zhou or find in the field).

**D. Briefing Room.** Where Hayes assigns missions. Holographic projector showing zone maps. 12 chairs, usually half empty (agents die or quit faster than they're replaced). The coffee machine hasn't worked since 2078. Nobody fixes it. It's a running joke and a metaphor.

**E. Infirmary.** Dr. Chen's domain. 6 beds, usually 3-4 occupied. Chen treats everyone regardless of faction or status. Healing takes 1 week (3 days at Loyalty 7+). If PCs befriend Chen, he shares information about contamination research. He's also the only person who can tell a PC how many Quirks they can sustain before echo transformation.

**Dilemma:** Chen asks a PC to volunteer for an experimental anti-contamination treatment. If it works: permanent +1 to WIL saves vs contamination. If it fails (50/50): gain 1 Fatigue permanently. Chen is honest about the odds. He's also desperate.

**F. Offices (Floors 2-5).** Bureaucratic labyrinth. Benson rules here. Any administrative request passes through him. Rodriguez's office is on Floor 3: messy, personal, a bottle of whiskey in the drawer. If PCs visit Rodriguez off-duty, he's more open. He'll warn them about upcoming dangerous missions before the official briefing, giving them time to prepare (or refuse).

**G. Hayes' Office (Floor 6).** Panoramic windows facing the epicenter. The Strip is always visible, shimmering and broken. Hayes sits with her back to the view. She doesn't look at it anymore. The office is clean, impersonal. One photo on the desk, face down. (Her daughter. If a PC asks, she changes the subject.) This is where promotions happen, where ultimatums are delivered, and where careers end.

**H. Basement (Restricted).** Requires Loyalty 8+ or a stolen access card (DEX save to use without detection). Contains: cells for "compromised" agents (1-3 prisoners at any time), classified archives (mission reports from the first 10 years after Oculus, heavily redacted), and the artifact vault (recovered Oculus fragments under armed guard). The archives contain proof that Hayes authorized "expendable" missions that killed 14 agents in 2076. Zhou would pay $10,000 for this information.

  │
[FLOORS 6-14] ─── 1934 hotel rooms, temporal ghosts, 1 echo
  │
[FLOORS 1-5] ─── Lobby, kitchens, service areas
  │
[BASEMENT] ─── Flooded, unstable, contains hidden vault

## The Sunken Strip (Red Zone Crawl)

**Zone:** Red. **Contamination:** WIL save every 3 hours, d4.
**Why PCs go here:** High-value artifact recovery, missing agent search, or Hayes orders it.

### Map

```
[A. PERIMETER] ─── [B. BOULEVARD]
                        │
              ┌─────────┼─────────┐
              │         │         │
       [C. LUXOR     [D. MGM    [E. BELLAGIO
        SHELL]      CRATER]     FOUNTAIN]
              │         │         │
              └─────────┼─────────┘
                        │
                 [F. THE SEAM]
                        │
                 [G. GROUND ZERO]
```

### Nodes

**A. Perimeter.** The boundary where Yellow becomes Red. The air tastes metallic. Shadows move wrong. Division marker posts every 100m, most damaged or displaced. A scavenger camp (3 scavengers, 4 HP, 10 STR, 12 DEX, 8 WIL, knife d6) offers to sell "safe routes" for $300. The routes are 70% accurate (WIL save to judge reliability).

**B. The Boulevard.** The former Las Vegas Boulevard. Buildings on both sides exist in 2-3 eras simultaneously. Walking down the center, you see the 1950s Flamingo on your left, the 2030s Wynn on your right, and something that hasn't been built yet directly ahead. The road surface changes every 20 meters: asphalt, cobblestone, bare earth, smooth future-material. 1 echo patrols here on a loop, walking the same 200m stretch endlessly.

**C. The Luxor Shell.** The black pyramid, or what remains of it. The top third is intact but phased: you can see through the walls to the sky beyond. The bottom two-thirds are solid but the interior shifts eras every 30 minutes. Inside: a massive atrium where the 1990s casino floor overlaps with a 2080 research lab. Slot machines next to quantum computers. 3 echoes (one wearing a lab coat, probably a former researcher). The research lab contains Division files from 2027: early Oculus containment protocols. Worth $5,000 to Hayes, $8,000 to Zhou.

**Dilemma:** The lab coat echo responds to its name (Dr. Sarah Voss). It doesn't attack. It says "Help me" repeatedly. It's pointing at a terminal. If PCs access the terminal (WIL save), they find Voss's final research notes: a theoretical cure for echo transformation. The notes are incomplete. Chen could complete them with 6 months of work. But the terminal is anchored to the Luxor. Removing it triggers structural collapse (everyone makes DEX save, d8 damage on failure). Leave the terminal? The cure stays theoretical. Destroy the building? The cure is lost forever.

**D. The MGM Crater.** A 50-meter-wide crater where the MGM Grand used to be. Now a bowl of temporal chaos: looking down, you see different time periods layered like geological strata. The 1960s at the rim, the 1920s in the middle, something prehistoric at the very bottom (is that a dinosaur skeleton?). Climbing down is possible but each "layer" requires a WIL save against contamination, d4 damage die. At the bottom: an intact Oculus fragment, class-A, worth $15,000+. Getting it out requires climbing back up through every layer.

**E. The Bellagio Fountain.** The famous fountains still run. But they spray temporal energy instead of water. Standing in the mist: WIL save or age d10 years (permanent, like the Quirk). The fountain area is safe from echoes (they avoid it, suggesting some awareness of self-preservation). This makes it a potential rest point, but resting here still triggers contamination checks. Around the fountain: 4 bodies of scavengers who didn't know about the aging mist. One has a treasure map (leads to the MGM Crater bottom).

**F. The Seam.** The boundary between Red and Black zones. A visible shimmer in the air, like heat haze but vertical. Crossing requires a STR save. Failure: d8 STR damage and you're thrown back. Success: you're in the Black Zone. Time feels thick here. Sound arrives late. Your movements leave afterimages that fade over 3 seconds. Everything is tinted blue-violet. Contamination check immediately upon crossing, d6 damage die.

**G. Ground Zero.** Directly beneath where the Oculus laboratory was. Now a crater within the crater, 20 meters deep. The air is still. Perfectly still. No wind, no sound, no temporal fluctuation. It's the eye of the storm. Contamination checks every hour, d8, but no suit reduction works here (the energy is too dense).

At the center: a pedestal of fused glass and metal. On it: the original Oculus device. Still running. Still breaking time. It hums at a frequency you feel in your teeth.

**This is the endgame location.** If the PCs reach here in the campaign's climax, they face the final choice: destroy it (vegas begins stabilizing, Division loses its purpose, Zhou loses her market), take it (unimaginable power, total corruption, everyone hunts you), or leave it (nothing changes, the zones keep expanding, more people die every year).
