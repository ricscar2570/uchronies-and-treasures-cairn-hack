## The Oculus Event (2025)

It was August 15, 2025, at 3:47 AM. A US government experiment on a technology called "Project Oculus," an attempt to manipulate time for military purposes, went catastrophically out of control. The secret laboratory under the Nevada desert imploded. But instead of exploding outward, it imploded *inward*, through time itself. The epicenter: the Luxor Hotel, Las Vegas Strip.

**Immediate consequences:**

- **0-5 km from the epicenter (the Strip):** Temporal annihilation. Everything that existed there vanished, replaced by a chaotic collage of different eras. The Luxor is now a superimposition of itself in 1920, 1950, 2025, and 2080. Simultaneously.
- **5-20 km (downtown Las Vegas):** Severe instability. Buildings "jump" between eras. People age or de-age randomly. Time flows at different speeds on adjacent streets.
- **20-50 km (outskirts):** Minor but persistent anomalies. Occasional temporal "echoes." Safe to inhabit but unsettling.

Estimated deaths: 400,000 in the first week. Another 200,000 in the following 5 years from contamination effects.

## The City's Geography (2080)

### Red Zone: The Epicenter (0-5 km)

**The Strip and the Luxor.** The most dangerous area. Completely temporally unstable.

- **Access:** Forbidden to civilians. Only Division agents with special authorization.
- **Contamination:** WIL save every **3 hours**. Damage die: **d4**.
- **Temporal events:** Every 30 minutes. Collapse possible at any moment.
- **Population:** Zero permanent. Only temporary recovery teams.
- **Treasures:** The greatest. Temporal artifacts, future technology, riches from the past.

### Yellow Zone: The Intermediate Ring (5-20 km)

**Downtown and the old neighborhoods.** Unstable but habitable with precautions.

- **Access:** Permitted with Division badge or temporary pass ($500/day).
- **Contamination:** WIL save every **8 hours**. Damage die: **d4**.
- **Temporal events:** Once a day. The Yellow Zone is safer than it looks, enough to live in, not enough to let your guard down.
- **Population:** ~50,000 people. Division workers, black market merchants, desperate people with no other options.
- **Services:** Zhou's black market, illegal clinics, agent bars, arsenals.

### Green Zone: The Safe Outskirts (20-50 km)

**The stabilized suburbs.** Almost normal life.

- **Access:** Free. No pass needed.
- **Contamination:** None. Occasional harmless temporal echoes.
- **Population:** ~300,000 people. Families, shops, schools, hospitals.
- **This is where agents live.** Apartments in the green zone, working in the red/yellow zones.

## The Economy of Vegas 2080

- **Temporal tourism:** People pay $10,000+ for guided tours in yellow zones. "See the past and future overlaid!"
- **Artifact recovery:** The main industry. Corporations pay millions for future technology recovered from red zones.
- **Black market:** Zhou controls 60% of the underground economy. Temporal certificates, weapons, fake identities.
- **The Temporal Division:** The largest employer. 15,000 employees.

## Atmosphere and Tone

Vegas is a city of violent contrasts: extreme wealth vs desperate poverty, futuristic technology vs ruins from the past, the Division's rigid legality vs the black market's anarchy, hope (is stabilization possible?) vs nihilism (is collapse inevitable?).

"Vegas was never an honest city. But at least before, the rules were clear. Now? Time itself is rigged."

**Visual inspirations:** Blade Runner 2049 (decay, contrasts), Fallout New Vegas (post-apocalyptic desert, abandoned casinos), The Expanse (dirty realism, grey factions), Dark on Netflix (temporal loops, consequences of choices).

> **Sample description for the table:**
>
> "Your car crosses the green zone checkpoint. Ahead, the sky changes. The yellow zone has a strange aura. The sunlight seems slightly wrong. Too intense in some spots, too dim in others. Buildings change architectural style every 50 meters: 1920s art deco, 1960s brutalism, 2080 futurism. All superimposed, all real.
>
> In the distance, always present: the Strip. A chaotic rainbow of colliding eras. The Luxor, black as obsidian, bright as glass, fragmented like a broken mirror. Beautiful. Terrifying.
>
> Hayes sent you here. Again. Until you die or become temporal echoes or accumulate enough debt to have no choice but to keep going.
>
> Welcome to Las Vegas. The city where time itself is rigged."

## The Temporal Division

### Structure

- **Director Hayes** (executive): Head of all Vegas operations.
- **Squad captains** (tactical): Coordinate 5-10 agents each.
- **Field agents** (operational): You. Enter red zones, recover artifacts, survive.
- **Support** (analysts, medics, techs): Stay at base, never enter dangerous zones.

### Philosophy

**"Stabilize, contain, recover."** Officially, the Division "protects the world." Unofficially, it's a government bureaucracy that underpays you, sends you on suicide missions, and expects blind loyalty.

### Headquarters

Former Aria Casino, green zone, 35 km from the epicenter. Ground floor: briefing room, armory, infirmary. Floors 2-5: offices, research labs. Floor 6: Director Hayes' office (panoramic view of the epicenter). Basements: cells for corrupt agents, classified archives, artifact storage.

## Madame Zhou and the Black Market

**Madame Zhou, "The Queen of the Yellow Zone."** A Chinese woman of about 60 (looks 40, illegal temporal surgery). Elegant, ruthless, pragmatic. Built a criminal empire on the ruins of Vegas.

**Her empire:** The Crimson Palace (former hotel, downtown fortress, nobody enters without invitation). Controls 80% of illegal temporal artifact trade. At least 30 Division agents on her payroll. 200+ armed mercenaries as guards.

She knows everything about everyone. Has infinite resources. Is patient (uses you, then discards you). Is charismatic (many agents prefer her to Hayes).

**Her secret goal:** Access to the original Project Oculus files in the Division's basements. She believes she can use that technology to "reset" the disaster. Or to create a monopoly on time itself.

## Key NPCs

### Director Elena Hayes

6 HP, 8 STR, 10 DEX, 16 WIL, concealed pistol (d6)

52 years old. White woman, grey hair pulled back, impeccable suit, tired eyes. Pragmatic, cold, efficient. Believes in the mission but cynical about bureaucracy. **Secret:** Was present during the Oculus event. Lost her daughter (age 8) at the epicenter. Works for the Division as penance. Sees PCs as necessary tools. Respects the loyal, eliminates the corrupt.

### Dr. Marcus Chen

3 HP, 6 STR, 8 DEX, 14 WIL, no weapons

38 years old. Asian man, thick glasses, stained lab coat, always exhausted. Head of medical research. Studies contamination, tries to cure Quirks. Idealistic, naive, genuinely wants to help. **Secret:** Has 3 Quirks (aging, memories of unlived lives, phase shift). Destroying himself to find a cure. Treats PCs for free if they're kind.

### Captain James "Ironside" Rodriguez

8 HP, 1 Armor, 14 STR, 12 DEX, 10 WIL, assault rifle (d8), combat knife (d6)

45 years old. Latino man, scars everywhere, missing left eye (black patch), gravelly voice. Veteran captain, 20 years in the Division. Tough, loyal, protective. "Bring everyone home." **Secret:** Refused promotion 5 times. Doesn't want to become like Hayes. Gruff mentor who covers for your mistakes. But if you betray the squad, he kills you personally.

### "Whisper" (Sofia Kovalenko)

6 HP, 1 Armor, 10 STR, 15 DEX, 12 WIL, suppressed pistol (d6), knife (d6)

29 years old. Slavic woman, short black hair, black tactical clothes, always armed. Freelance agent for Zhou. Pragmatic to the extreme, loyal only to herself. **Secret:** Former Division agent (Veteran tier). Left after Hayes let her team die on a "sacrificial" mission. Potential ally or enemy. Depends on how much you pay.

### Tommy "Dice" Martinez

4 HP, 8 STR, 10 DEX, 14 WIL, hidden derringer (d4)

52 years old. Latino man, red velvet jacket, oily smile. Manager of the "Lucky 38" bar/casino in the yellow zone. Friendly, talkative, professional informant. **Secret:** Triple agent. Spies for Zhou, Hayes, and anyone who pays. No loyalty but too useful to eliminate. Sells PCs info ($100-$500). Won't betray you directly but sells your info to others.

## Other Notable NPCs

> These NPCs are shaped by the absurdity of post-Oculus Vegas. Each is tragic, each is strange, and each is **mechanically useful** to the party.

### "Double" Danny Chen

4 HP, 8 STR, 8 DEX, 6 WIL

40 years old (apparent). Asian-American in a 1950s grey suit, semi-transparent in direct sunlight (Echo), carries 3 wallets with different family photos. Convinced he is the brother/cousin/relative of ANYONE he meets. Pre-Oculus private investigator, transformed into an Echo with identity mutation.

**Running gag:** "BROTHER! It's been so long! Mom was asking about you just yesterday!"

**Mechanical use:** Perfectly remembers every building and street of pre-Oculus Las Vegas. Can locate hidden entrances, buried bank vaults, secret bunkers. WIL save to follow his directions. Limitation: knows nothing post-2027.

**Location:** "Danny's Corner," Fremont Street, stuck in a 1958 loop.

**Mission hook:** Danny asks help closing his last case. The woman he was searching for is still trapped in a Red Zone, alive in temporal stasis. If PCs save her, Danny has a moment of lucidity, thanks them, and disappears (loop resolved). If they fail, Danny adds the PCs to his "invented family memories."

### Sergeant Benson "The Bureaucrat"

5 HP, 10 STR, 6 DEX, 12 WIL

50 years old. Overweight, balding, thick glasses, tight tie even in hot zones, clipboard and stamps everywhere. Division administrative sergeant. Genuinely believes bureaucracy is the only defense against temporal chaos.

**Running gag:** Any request = specific form required. Ammunition? Form 16-C. Report an Echo? Form 44-E. Building collapsing? "Emergency procedure: fill out Form 99-EMERGENCY. Section is on fire? Use the carbon copy."

**Mechanical use:** Master of the system. Ways to get favors: fill forms perfectly (1 hour downtime, WIL save), find a regulatory loophole (WIL save), or bribe him with **vintage forms** from Red Zones (he collects them). Favors: archive access, extra budget (+$200), "losing" negative reports, fast-track promotions. Never breaks rules openly but knows every loophole.

**Location:** Division basement office. Nobody goes there voluntarily.

**Mission hook:** Pre-Oculus Division archives trapped in a Red Zone. Birth/death records, the only way for families to verify if relatives are dead or missing. Zhou wants the same archives (blackmail material). Player choice: Hayes or Zhou?

**Heartbreaking moment:** PCs return injured. Benson puts away his forms. "The forms can wait. Come, I have a first aid kit. Don't get killed out there. The paperwork for agent death is... terrible." He lost his son in the Oculus Event. Bureaucracy is his way of maintaining control.

### "Lucky" Lorenzo Vitale

3 HP, 8 STR, 12 DEX, 10 WIL

30 years old. Italian-American, casual clothes, lucky charms everywhere, diagonal scar across face, permanent smile. Red Zone scavenger with unexplainable supernatural luck.

Pre-Oculus Bellagio croupier. During the Event, everyone at Ground Zero died. He tripped, fell into an open manhole, emerged 3 days later unscathed. Luck completely inverted.

**Running gag:** Incapable of perceiving danger. Not brave, just doesn't understand he should be afraid. Buildings collapse behind him. Echoes try to attack him and trip over each other. Gravity anomalies don't affect him. "Problems? I don't see problems."

**Mechanical use:** Best scavenger in Las Vegas. Can "sense" treasures (reads temporal anomalies unconsciously). Treasure maps ($500 or a favor, d3 locations at 90% accuracy). If Lorenzo accompanies PCs in a Red Zone, group gets **advantage on saves** against temporal hazards (rifts, gravity anomalies, time loops) but NOT against routine contamination checks. Also trades artifacts and has intel on Zhou.

**Location:** "Lorenzo's Luck," a reinforced shack at the Yellow/Red Zone edge. Inexplicably stable.

**Mission hook:** Lorenzo wants to visit Ground Zero. A recurring dream calls him there. Twist: he's tied to a buried Oculus Fragment, temporally "anchored." Each time he dies, the timeline resets just for him. If PCs remove the Fragment: he becomes normal (loses luck, gains peace). If they leave it: lucky but tormented. If they give it to Hayes/Zhou: Lorenzo stays tied, faction gains power. No right answer.

## Adapting the Setting

Las Vegas 2080 is the default setting, but the mechanics work anywhere with three elements: a **contaminated zone** with a danger gradient, an **unsustainable economy** that pushes toward corruption, and an **ambiguous authority** sending desperate people to do dangerous work.

**Tokyo 2080.** Oculus hit Shibuya. Temporal Yakuza replaces Zhou. Division operates from Yokota base. Japanese social pressure amplifies shame of corruption.

**Berlin 1989.** The Wall is a temporal anomaly. The Stasi is the Division. Crossing the Wall ages or de-ages you by decades. Smugglers operate in tunnels under Checkpoint Charlie.

**Naples 2080.** Oculus hit Vesuvius, which now erupts fragments of time. The Camorra is Zhou. Historic center is Red Zone, Spanish Quarters are Yellow Zone.

**An oil platform in the Gulf of Mexico.** Closed space, no escape. Contamination from the seabed. The oil company is the Division. The shift supervisor is Hayes. The Mexican cook is Zhou.

To adapt: change the names, keep the functions. You need a Hayes (authority that sends you to die), a Zhou (temptation that corrupts you), a Red Zone (where nobody wants to go but where the loot is), and rent you can't pay.
