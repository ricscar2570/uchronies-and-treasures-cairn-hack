# Temporal Relics
{: .no_toc }

Temporal relics are artifacts recovered from the zones. They are not weapons or tools in the ordinary sense. They are fragments of broken time made solid. Each one bends reality in a specific way, costs something to use, and is worth a fortune on the black market.

Relics take up 1 inventory slot unless noted. They have **charges** and a **recharge condition**. Using a relic never requires a save unless specified.

---

**Stillwatch.** 3 charges. A cracked pocket watch that freezes time in a 10-foot radius for 6 seconds (1 round). Everything inside the radius stops: bullets, people, falling debris. You are frozen too. Useful for avoiding an explosion or stopping a bleeding wound long enough to apply pressure. Recharge: submerge in running water for 1 hour.

**Rewind Syringe.** 1 charge. A glass syringe filled with shimmering blue liquid. Inject into yourself or a willing target. The target's body resets to its state 6 hours ago: wounds heal (restore all HP and STR), contamination damage is undone, but memories of those 6 hours are lost completely. The target doesn't remember what happened. One use. Incredibly valuable. Zhou pays $8,000. The Division wants it for research.

**Echo Lantern.** 3 charges. A battered oil lantern that reveals temporal echoes within 50 feet, even invisible or phased ones. Echoes glow faintly in the lantern light. Also reveals hidden temporal rifts and unstable zones. Does not affect echoes or make them hostile. Recharge: leave lit in complete darkness for 24 hours.

**Gravity Glove.** 2 charges. A heavy black glove with copper wiring. Touch an object (up to 200 lbs). For the next minute, the object's gravity reverses: it falls upward. Useful for clearing barricades, creating distractions, or trapping enemies under their own furniture. Does not work on living creatures. Recharge: drop the glove from a height of at least 30 feet.

**Chrono Grenade.** 1 charge. Looks like a standard frag grenade with an amber crystal core. On detonation, everything in a 20-foot radius ages 10 years instantly. Metal rusts, wood rots, concrete crumbles. Living creatures in the blast: STR save or take d8 STR damage (aging shock). Does NOT affect temporal echoes (they exist outside normal time). One use. Cannot be recharged. Worth $3,000 to Zhou.

**The Listener.** 3 charges. A small earpiece made of bone-white material. When activated, you hear conversations that happened in your current location in the past 72 hours. You choose which time window to listen to (within those 72 hours). Useful for intelligence gathering, discovering what happened to a missing squad, or learning Zhou's plans in a room she used recently. Recharge: spend 1 hour in complete silence (no talking, no movement).

**Phase Ring.** 2 charges. A plain iron ring. When worn and activated, your hand becomes intangible for 10 seconds. You can reach through walls, locked doors, or sealed containers. You can grab one object on the other side and pull it back (the object phases with your hand). Cannot reach through more than 1 foot of material. If the charge runs out while your hand is inside a solid object: d10 STR damage (your hand re-materializes inside the wall). Recharge: the ring must be left inside a locked container overnight.

**Deja Vu Engine.** 1 charge. A fist-sized brass device covered in tiny gears. When activated, rewinds the last 10 seconds for everyone in a 30-foot radius. Everyone except the user forgets those 10 seconds happened. The user remembers everything. Effectively a "save point": if a fight goes wrong, activate it and try a different approach. Enemies don't remember attacking you. Allies don't remember the plan that failed. Recharge: the user must experience a genuine surprise (not manufactured) while carrying the device.

**Temporal Compass.** No charges (passive). A compass where the needle points toward the nearest Oculus fragment instead of north. The stronger the fragment, the harder the needle pulls. Does not indicate distance, only direction. Takes up 0 slots (pocket-sized). Worth $5,000 to Zhou (she has agents searching for fragments). Worth $2,000 to the Division (they already have detection equipment, but this is portable and silent). Carrying this in a Red Zone: echoes are drawn to you (they sense the compass resonance). Encounter checks are rolled with disadvantage.

**Dr. Voss's Injector.** 3 charges. A medical injector labeled "TEMPORAL STABILIZATION - PROTOTYPE - NOT FOR HUMAN USE." Reduces contamination damage by 2 (stacks with suit) for 6 hours after injection. Side effect: vivid hallucinations of alternate timelines for 1 hour after the effect ends (WIL save or lose next turn in combat during hallucination period). Recharge: refill with anti-rejection medicine (consumes 1 week of medicine supply, $250).

**The Black Mirror.** 1 charge. A hand mirror with a frame of dark metal. Look into it and speak a person's name. The mirror shows that person's current location and situation for 30 seconds. Works across any distance. Does NOT work on temporal echoes or the dead. Zhou uses one of these to keep tabs on her agents. If PCs find one, they can spy on Zhou, Hayes, or anyone else. Recharge: break the mirror. It reassembles itself over 7 days.

**Anchor Spike.** 2 charges. A foot-long iron spike covered in etched symbols. Drive it into the ground: for the next hour, temporal anomalies within 20 feet are suppressed. No random temporal events, no gravity shifts, no time loops. Contamination checks still apply (the spike anchors space, not biological exposure). Useful for creating a safe camp in a Red Zone. If an echo touches the spike, it is forced into the current timeline (becomes solid and targetable, loses the ability to phase). Recharge: leave the spike at the epicenter of a temporal event for 1 hour.
