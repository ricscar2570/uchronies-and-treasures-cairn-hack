## Character Creation

> **Target time: 15 minutes.**
> You don't build an optimal character after 2 hours of analysis. You generate a desperate person ready to play immediately.

> **A note on the learning curve.** Uchronies & Treasures has six trackers per character. It is not a minimal-rules game: it is a game with few resolution mechanics and many narrative variables to keep in balance. Character creation takes 15 minutes. Feeling comfortable with all the trackers takes two sessions. This is normal and by design: each tracker becomes interesting at the moment it starts to bite.

### Overview (7 Steps)

1. **Roll Ability Scores** (1 minute)
2. **Roll Hit Protection** (30 seconds)
3. **Choose or roll Background** (2 minutes)
4. **Note starting gear** (2 minutes)
5. **Set trackers** (1 minute)
6. **Roll traits** (2 minutes)
7. **Narrative details** (3 minutes)

### 1. Ability Scores

Player Characters (PCs) have three attributes:

- **Strength (STR):** Physical power, endurance, resistance to poison and pain.
- **Dexterity (DEX):** Agility, reflexes, precision, stealth.
- **Willpower (WIL):** Mental resilience, perception, social influence. Resisting contamination, persuading, deceiving, commanding.

**Roll 3d6 for each attribute, in order.** You may then swap any two results.

*Example: Marco rolls 14 STR, 8 DEX, 12 WIL. He swaps STR and DEX, ending with 8 STR, 14 DEX, 12 WIL. An agile but physically weak agent.*

### 2. Hit Protection (HP)

**Roll 1d6** to determine your starting **Hit Protection (HP)**.

HP reflects your ability to avoid serious harm: luck, reflexes, instinct for cover. It does **not** represent physical toughness. When HP reaches 0, the next hit goes directly to your body (STR damage).

HP recovers quickly (a few moments of rest and water). Attribute damage takes a week of care to heal.

### 3. Background

**Choose deliberately or roll 1d6.** Your background determines your starting gear package and tells the table who you *were* before the Division recruited you.

In Cairn, backgrounds don't grant mechanical skills. Instead, they inform what your character would plausibly know and what approaches make narrative sense. The Warden uses your background to determine what information you'd have and what actions don't require a save.

| d6 | Background | You were... |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ex-Military** | A soldier, marine, or private contractor. Discharged (injury? PTSD? end of contract?). You know weapons, tactics, and survival. |
| 2 | **Failed Academic** | A doctoral candidate in history, physics, or archaeology. Never finished (funding cut? scandal? burnout?). You know languages, epochs, and artifacts. |
| 3 | **Reformed Criminal** | A burglar, forger, or worse. You got caught. The Division offered a job instead of prison. "Reformed" is a strong word. |
| 4 | **Corrupt Cop** | A detective who took bribes. Everyone did, right? You got caught. Fired. The Division hires people who already know the grey zone. |
| 5 | **Disbarred Medic** | You had a medical license. A mistake took it away. The Division doesn't ask for references. They ask for competence. |
| 6 | **Con Artist** | You sold air, false promises, worthless investments. Good with words, better with lies. |


### Starting Gear

All agents begin with:

**Standard Division issue (5 slots used):**

- Glock 17 pistol (d6 damage, 1 slot)
- 2 extra magazines (1 slot, bundled)
- Basic first aid kit (3 uses, heals d4 STR loss, 1 slot)
- Anti-rejection medicine, 4 weeks supply (1 slot)
- 3 days rations (1 slot)

**Worn (not counted toward slots):**

- Light tactical vest, kevlar II (1 Armor)
- Temporal protection suit (reduces WIL damage from contamination by 1, worn under clothes)
- Civilian clothes

**Pockets (0 slots):**

- Encrypted cellphone
- Division badge
- $500 cash

### Inventory

Characters have **10 inventory slots**: a backpack with six slots, one slot for each hand, and two slots on the body (belt, vest, holster).

Most items take up **one slot**. Small items can be bundled together. **Bulky** items take **two slots** and are awkward to carry.

A character with a full inventory (all 10 slots occupied) is reduced to **0 HP**. You can't carry more than your inventory allows.

> **Tension by design:** the anti-rejection medicine takes 1 slot. Your gun takes 1 slot. Every piece of loot you take from a time zone is a slot you can't use for something else. Inventory management *is* the game's resource pressure alongside money.
