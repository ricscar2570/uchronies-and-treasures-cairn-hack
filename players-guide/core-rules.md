## Core Rules

### Saves

A **save** is a roll to avoid bad outcomes from risky choices and circumstances. Roll a **d20**. If you roll **equal to or under** the relevant attribute, you succeed. Otherwise, you fail. A **1** is always a success. A **20** is always a failure.

When a save is made with **advantage**, roll 2d20 and keep the **lower** result (better for the player). When made with **disadvantage**, roll 2d20 and keep the **higher** result (worse for the player).

**STR saves:** Resisting poison, enduring pain, forcing a door, absorbing a physical impact.

**DEX saves:** Dodging an explosion, acting before an enemy, sneaking past guards, keeping your balance.

**WIL saves:** Resisting temporal contamination, seeing through a lie, maintaining composure under interrogation, persuading someone who doesn't trust you.

> When no save is needed, the Warden simply decides based on the fiction. If your background as an ex-military means you'd know how to field-strip a weapon, you just do it. No roll.

### Healing

**HP** recovers after a few moments of rest and a drink of water. This leaves the party exposed (the Warden may trigger an encounter).

**Attribute damage** (STR, DEX, or WIL loss) requires **a week of rest** facilitated by a healer or medical care. Division medical services are available but slow (1 week). At Loyalty 7+, priority care restores attributes in 3 days. Black market healing is faster but costs $200-$500.

A character who lacks a crucial need (food, water, rest, anti-rejection medicine) is **Deprived**. A Deprived character cannot recover HP or attributes.

### Fatigue

Anyone Deprived for more than a day adds **Fatigue** to their inventory, one per day. Each Fatigue occupies one inventory slot and lasts until the character recovers in safety (full night's rest in a safe place).

Temporal contamination and certain Quirk effects can also cause Fatigue.

### Armor

Before calculating damage to HP, subtract the target's **Armor** value from the damage roll. No one can have more than **3 Armor** total, regardless of how many pieces of protection they wear.

### Reactions

When the PCs encounter an NPC whose reaction is not obvious, the Warden rolls **2d6**:

### Morale

Enemies must make a **WIL save** to avoid fleeing when they take their **first casualty** and again when they lose **half their number**. Groups may use their leader's WIL. Lone foes save when reduced to 0 HP. Morale does not apply to PCs.

## Combat

### Rounds

When timing matters, use **rounds** (roughly 10 seconds). On their turn, a character may move up to **40 feet** and take **one action**: attack, use an item, move again, or any other reasonable action.

### Initiative

At the start of combat, each PC must make a **DEX save** to act before their opponents. PCs who fail lose their first turn. After the first round, turns alternate: PCs, then enemies.

Special circumstances (ambush, superior position) may let one side act automatically first.

### Attacking and Damage

**Attacks automatically hit.** The attacker rolls their weapon's damage die and subtracts the target's Armor. The remainder is applied to the target's HP.

**Unarmed attacks** always deal **d4** damage.

*Example: Reyes fires her Glock 17 (d6) at a scavenger wearing a leather jacket (1 Armor). She rolls a 5. Minus 1 Armor = 4 damage to the scavenger's HP.*

### Multiple Attackers

If multiple attackers target the same foe, roll all damage dice and keep the **single highest result**.

### Dual Weapons

If attacking with two weapons at the same time, roll both damage dice and keep the **single highest result**.

### Attack Modifiers

**Impaired:** Fighting from a position of weakness (behind cover, restrained, blinded). Roll **d4** damage regardless of weapon.

**Enhanced:** Fighting from a position of advantage (point-blank against helpless target, ambush). Roll **d12** damage regardless of weapon.

### Blast

Attacks with the **blast** quality affect all targets in the noted area. Roll damage separately for each. Explosives, grenades, and certain temporal anomalies have blast.

### Critical Damage

Damage that reduces a target's HP **below zero** applies the remainder to **STR**. The target must then make a **STR save** to avoid **critical damage**.

A character who suffers critical damage collapses. They can only crawl weakly. If given aid within an hour, they stabilize. If left untreated, they die.

*Example: Chen has 2 HP remaining. A guard shoots him with a rifle (d8) and rolls 6. After subtracting 1 Armor, that's 5 damage. 2 HP absorbed, 3 remaining goes to STR. Chen must make a STR save. His STR is 11. He rolls 14. Failure. Chen collapses, bleeding. His teammates have one hour.*

### Attribute Loss

If **STR** reaches 0: **death**. If **DEX** reaches 0: **paralysis**. If **WIL** reaches 0 from contamination: **temporal echo** (lost). If **WIL** reaches 0 otherwise: **catatonia**.

### Scars

When damage reduces HP to **exactly 0**, the character is marked. Roll on the Scars table based on **total damage taken**:

  ---- -----------------------------------------------------------------
  1    **Lasting Scar.** Roll d6: 1 Neck, 2 Hands, 3 Eye, 4 Chest,
       5 Legs, 6 Ear. Roll d6. If higher than max HP, take new result.

  2    **Rattling Blow.** Disoriented and shaken. Roll d6. If higher
       than max HP, take new result.

  3    **Winded.** Sent flying. Deprived until you rest a few hours.
       Then roll d6, add to max HP.

  4    **Broken Limb.** Roll d6: 1-2 Leg, 3-4 Arm, 5 Rib, 6 Skull.
       Once mended, roll 2d6. If higher than max HP, take new result.

  5    **Infected.** Gross infection. When you recover, roll 2d6.
       If higher than max HP, take new result.

  6    **Head Wound.** Roll d6: 1-2 STR, 3-4 DEX, 5-6 WIL. Roll 3d6.
       If higher than current ability, take new result.

  7    **Hamstrung.** Can barely move without help. After recovery,
       roll 3d6. If higher than max DEX, take new result.

  8    **Deafened.** Cannot hear until extraordinary aid. Make WIL save.
       Pass: increase max WIL by d4.

  9    **Concussed.** Part of your psyche knocked loose. Roll 3d6.
       If higher than max WIL, take new result.

  10   **Dismembered.** An appendage torn off or crippled. Warden
       decides which. Make WIL save. Pass: increase max WIL by d6.

  11   **Mortal Wound.** Deprived and out of action. Die in one hour
       unless healed. On recovery, roll 2d6 as new max HP.

  12   **Doomed.** You survived, barely. If your next critical damage
       save fails, you die horribly. Pass: roll 3d6. If higher than
       max HP, take new result.
  ---- -----------------------------------------------------------------

### Detachments

Large groups of similar combatants fight as a single **detachment**. When a detachment takes critical damage, it is **routed**. When it reaches 0 STR, it is **destroyed**.

Attacks against detachments by individuals are **impaired** (d4, unless blast). Attacks by detachments against individuals are **enhanced** (d12) and deal **blast** damage.

### Retreat

Running from a dangerous situation requires a successful **DEX save** and a safe destination to run to.

### Death and Replacement

When a character dies, the player creates a new character or takes control of a hireling. The new character joins the party immediately to minimize downtime.

The Warden and the player decide how: a Division replacement arriving with the next supply drop, a scavenger who decides to join the team, or a prisoner freed during the mission.
