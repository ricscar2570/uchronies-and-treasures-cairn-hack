## Core Rules

### Saves

A **save** is a roll to avoid bad outcomes from risky choices and circumstances. Roll a **d20**. If you roll **equal to or under** the relevant attribute, you succeed. Otherwise, you fail. A **1** is always a success. A **20** is always a failure.

When a save is made with **advantage**, roll 2d20 and keep the **lower** result (better for the player). When made with **disadvantage**, roll 2d20 and keep the **higher** result (worse for the player).

**STR saves:** Resisting poison, enduring pain, forcing a door, absorbing a physical impact.

**DEX saves:** Dodging an explosion, acting before an enemy, sneaking past guards, keeping your balance.

**WIL saves:** Resisting temporal contamination, seeing through a lie, maintaining composure under interrogation, persuading someone who doesn't trust you.

> When no save is needed, the Warden simply decides based on the fiction. If your background as an ex-military means you'd know how to field-strip a weapon, you just do it. No roll.

### Healing

**HP** recovers after a few moments of rest and a drink of water. This leaves the party exposed (the Warden may trigger an encounter).

**Attribute damage** (STR, DEX, or WIL loss) requires **a week of rest** facilitated by a healer or medical care. Division medical services are available but slow (1 week). At Loyalty 7+, priority care restores attributes in 3 days. Black market healing is faster but costs $200-$500.

A character who lacks a crucial need (food, water, rest, anti-rejection medicine) is **Deprived**. A Deprived character cannot recover HP or attributes.

### Fatigue

Anyone Deprived for more than a day adds **Fatigue** to their inventory, one per day. Each Fatigue occupies one inventory slot and lasts until the character recovers in safety (full night's rest in a safe place).

Temporal contamination and certain Quirk effects can also cause Fatigue.

### Armor

Before calculating damage to HP, subtract the target's **Armor** value from the damage roll. No one can have more than **3 Armor** total, regardless of how many pieces of protection they wear.
