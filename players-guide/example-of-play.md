# Example of Play

This scene shows a typical session opening: the weekly accounting, a briefing, a short exploration, and Zhou's offer. The players are **Reyes** (Ex-Military, STR 12, DEX 14, WIL 9, 4 HP) and **Marco** (Con Artist, STR 8, DEX 11, WIL 13, 3 HP). It's Week 4. Both are Recruits.

---

**Warden:** "Monday morning. Payday. Let's do the books."

**Warden:** *(writing on the whiteboard)* "Reyes: $800 pay. Expenses: rent $500, food $150, medicine $250. That's $900 out, $800 in. Balance: minus $100. Your debt was $420 from last week. Now it's $520. Marco, same math. Your debt was $380. Now it's $480."

**Marco:** "Every week. Every single week. Can we at least get some overtime?"

**Warden:** "Hayes doesn't do overtime. But she does have a mission. Briefing room, 07:00."

---

**Warden:** *(as Hayes)* "Agents. A warehouse in the Yellow Zone, 14 km out. We lost contact with a sensor team yesterday. Objective: find the team, recover their data chip, get out. You have the standard suit. Bonus: $150 base plus $200 if the data comes back intact."

**Reyes:** "Any hostile contacts?"

**Warden:** "Hayes checks her tablet. 'Scavenger activity in the area. No confirmed echoes. The zone is Yellow, so contamination checks every 8 hours. You should be in and out in 4. Questions? No? Move out.'"

**Marco:** "What's the team's last known position?"

**Warden:** "She sends coordinates to your phones. A warehouse complex off Fremont, near Danny's Corner. You know the area."

---

**Warden:** "You arrive at the warehouse complex. Two buildings, connected by a covered walkway. Building A has its doors open. Building B is sealed shut, metal shutters down. The air is thick. Time feels sluggish here. You notice the shadows of the buildings don't quite match the sun's position."

**Reyes:** "I check Building A first. Weapon drawn."

**Warden:** "Inside: a large open space, shelving units, dust. Near the back wall, you see Division equipment. Two tactical vests on the ground. A smashed radio. Blood trail leading toward the walkway to Building B."

**Marco:** "How much blood?"

**Warden:** "Enough that someone was hurt badly but walked away. The trail is smeared, like they were dragging themselves or being dragged."

**Reyes:** "I follow the blood trail. Moving carefully."

**Warden:** "You reach the walkway. It's dim. Through the window, you can see Building B's interior flickering. One moment it's a modern warehouse. Next moment it's a 1950s storage facility with wooden crates. The temporal overlap is active."

**Marco:** "I try the shutters on Building B."

**Warden:** "Locked from inside. You'd need to force them."

**Marco:** "I'm a con artist, not a locksmith. Can I find another way in?"

**Warden:** "You notice a ventilation grate on the side of the walkway. Big enough to crawl through. It leads into Building B's ceiling space."

**Marco:** "I go through the vent. Quietly."

**Warden:** "The building is flickering between eras. Make a DEX save to time your movement between the flickers."

**Marco:** *(rolls d20: 8, under his DEX of 11)* "Made it."

**Warden:** "You emerge on a catwalk above the warehouse floor. Below: two scavengers, armed, going through Division supply crates. One has a pistol, the other a crowbar. Between them, on the floor: Agent Dominguez, Division sensor tech. Alive, hands zip-tied, bruised face. She sees you. Her eyes go wide. She doesn't make a sound."

**Reyes:** "How many exits does the building have?"

**Warden:** "The shuttered front entrance, the walkway where you came from, and a loading dock on the far side. The loading dock is open."

**Reyes:** "I go around to the loading dock. I'll come in from behind while Marco has the high ground."

**Warden:** "You circle the building. As you approach the loading dock, you hear the scavengers talking."

**Warden:** *(as Scavenger 1)* "'...worth at least two grand to Zhou. Division data chip, fresh sensor readings. She'll pay double if we bring the agent too. Living agents are worth more than dead ones.'"

**Marco:** *(whispering)* "So they work for Zhou. Interesting."

**Reyes:** "I don't care who they work for. Dominguez is Division. We get her out. I step into the loading dock, gun raised. 'Division. Drop your weapons.'"

**Warden:** "The scavengers spin around. Initiative. Reyes, DEX save."

**Reyes:** *(rolls d20: 6, under DEX 14)* "I go first."

**Warden:** "Marco, DEX save."

**Marco:** *(rolls d20: 15, over DEX 11)* "Failed. I lose my first turn."

**Warden:** "Reyes, you act first. The scavengers are 30 feet away, partially behind crates."

**Reyes:** "I shoot the one with the pistol. He's the bigger threat."

**Warden:** "Roll damage."

**Reyes:** *(rolls d6: 5)* "Five."

**Warden:** "Scavenger has no armor. Five damage to his 4 HP. That drops him below zero. One point carries over to STR. He makes a STR save..."  *(rolls d20: 17, over STR 10)* "Failed. Critical damage. He collapses, screaming, clutching his shoulder. The other scavenger sees his partner go down. Morale check."  *(rolls d20 for WIL save: 14, over WIL 8)* "Failed. He drops the crowbar and runs for the loading dock."

**Marco:** "I shout from the catwalk: 'There are Division agents everywhere! You're surrounded!'"

**Warden:** "He doesn't need convincing. He's gone. Dominguez is on the floor, zip-tied, looking up at you."

---

**Warden:** "You cut Dominguez free. She's shaken but functional. She hands you the data chip. 'Thank you. Those bastards jumped us at dawn. Kowalski didn't make it.' She points to a corner you hadn't checked. Agent Kowalski is there. Dead. Crowbar wound to the head."

**Reyes:** *(silence)*

**Marco:** "We take the chip and go. How long have we been in zone?"

**Warden:** "About 3 hours. Well under the 8-hour Yellow Zone check. You're clear on contamination."

---

**Warden:** "Back at base. Debriefing with Hayes. You hand over the chip. She nods. 'Good work. Kowalski's death is... unfortunate. His family will be notified.' She pauses. 'Bonus: $150 base plus $200 for the chip. Dominguez confirms your performance. Dismissed.'"

**Warden:** "Accounting update. $350 bonus this week. Your deficit was $100. Net gain: $250. Reyes, your debt goes from $520 to $270. Marco, $480 to $230."

**Marco:** "Finally breathing."

**Warden:** "You get home that evening. There's a note in your mailbox. Perfumed paper, handwritten."

**Marco:** "Oh no."

**Warden:** *(reading)* "'Dear Agent Marco, I understand you encountered some of my associates today in the Yellow Zone. No hard feelings. Business is business. I notice your financial situation remains... challenging. I have a small task. $1,500 for one evening. Nothing dangerous. My address is the Crimson Palace. You know where it is. With regards, M. Zhou.'"

**Marco:** *(long pause)* "What's my debt again?"

**Warden:** "$230."

**Marco:** "And next week's deficit?"

**Warden:** "$100. So by next Monday you're at $330 debt. Unless you do a mission."

**Marco:** *(to Reyes)* "What would you do?"

**Reyes:** "I'd tell Zhou to go to hell."

**Marco:** "Easy for you to say. Your Loyalty is 7. Mine is 5. And my mother's medicine costs $300 a week on top of everything else."

**Warden:** "Zhou's note is sitting on your kitchen table. The rent bill is next to it. What do you do?"

*(End of scene)*
