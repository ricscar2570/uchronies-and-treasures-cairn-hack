> A **4-session** mini-campaign (2-3 hours each) showing how all systems interact: economy, contamination, loyalty, corruption, and moral choices. Unlike the Chicago Loop (single adventure), this campaign makes you **feel time passing.** Debt grows. Zhou knocks. Contamination accumulates. Characters change, literally.

## Preparation

PCs are freshly arrived recruits. Create them normally. The Warden keeps an **accounting sheet** for each PC: pay, expenses, debt, loyalty, corruption, contamination, Quirks. Update between sessions. Players must see the numbers. The spiral is more effective when it's transparent.

## Session 1: The Arrival (Weeks 1-2)

### Scene 1: Welcome Briefing

Hayes receives the PCs in her office. Efficient, cold, professional. Key information:

- Pay: $800/week, paid Monday
- Expenses: rent $500, food $150, anti-rejection medicine $250. Total: $900
- The deficit is $100/week. Hayes doesn't mention it. PCs discover it when they do the math
- Standard equipment distributed
- First mission tomorrow

> Hayes isn't evil. She's a bureaucrat doing her job. When a PC asks "how do you survive on $800 a week?", Hayes answers: "With discipline and priorities, agent." She's not lying. It simply isn't her problem.

### Scene 2: First Mission (Yellow Zone)

**Objective:** Recover a crate of biological samples from a warehouse in the Yellow Zone, 12 km from base.

**The zone:** Semi-abandoned residential neighborhood. Buildings "jump" between eras. The air is thick. Time flows irregularly.

**Contamination:** 1 WIL save after 8 hours in zone (Yellow Zone standard). With the suit (reduces WIL damage by 1), risk is low but real.

**Complication (Warden's choice):**

- **Option A (social):** Inside the warehouse is a civilian family taking shelter. They have medicine. The crate is under their belongings. Taking it leaves them without shelter.
- **Option B (tactical):** The warehouse is watched by 2 armed scavengers (Yellow Zone bandits, 4 HP, 10 STR, 12 DEX, 8 WIL, pistol d6). Fight is risky. Negotiate is possible. Flanking costs time.
- **Option C (moral):** The crate also contains a small unregistered Oculus fragment. Nobody knows it's there. Worth $3,000 on the black market. PCs discover it during transport.

**End of mission:** Debriefing with Hayes. Base bonus $150 (net). If objective completed: +$200 (after deductions: ~$140). +1 Loyalty if they hand everything over.

**Week 1 accounting:** Pay $800 + bonus ~$290 = $1,090. Expenses $900. Balance: +$190. Debt: -$310 (starting -$500 plus the $190 surplus). PCs feel almost good. Almost.

**Week 2:** No mission. Pay $800 only. Expenses $900. Unexpected expense (roll d6): say $100 (car/phone problem). Week balance: -$200. Total debt: -$510. PCs realize that one week without a mission digs the hole deeper.

## Session 2: The Pressure (Weeks 3-4)

### Scene 1: Two Missions Offered

Hayes offers two missions. PCs can choose one (can't do both, same time window).

**Mission A: Yellow Zone, routine.** Escort a Division tech installing temporal seismic sensors. Low risk. Base bonus $150, mission bonus $100.

**Mission B: Red Zone, dangerous.** Data recovery from a collapsed lab on the Strip. 3 confirmed temporal echoes. Base bonus $150, mission bonus $400, red zone bonus $200. But contamination is severe (WIL save every 3 hours, d4 damage die, 2 checks guaranteed).

The choice defines the session's tone. Mission B pays triple but a Quirk is nearly guaranteed.

### Scene 2: Zhou's Offer

If any PC's debt exceeds -$700 (it will by week 4 without extra missions), **Zhou makes contact.** Not in person: a handwritten note on perfumed paper in their mailbox.

> "Dear Agent [name], I've noticed the Division doesn't sufficiently appreciate your services. I have a small assignment that could solve your financial problems. Nothing dangerous. Nothing illegal (technically). $1,500 for one hour of your time. You already know my address. Everyone in Vegas does.
>
> With regards, M. Zhou"

If the PC goes: the job is simple. Carry a sealed package from point A to point B. Don't open it, don't ask questions. Pay: $1,500 cash. Corruption: +1.

If the PC refuses, Zhou doesn't insist. She waits. The offers get worse as debt grows.

### Scene 3: Side Gig

Benson casually mentions a Yellow Zone bar looking for a weekend bouncer. $200 cash. No particular risk, but you sacrifice downtime (no HP recovery, no socializing).

**Accounting weeks 3-4:** If they did Mission A (safe): debt growing, around -$600 by end of session 2. If Mission B (risky): probably a Quirk, but better financially (~-$350). If someone accepted Zhou (+$1,500): debt nearly cleared, but Corruption at 1. Instability looms.

## Session 3: The Fall (Weeks 5-7)

This is the session where the game bares its teeth.

### Scene 1: The Mission Goes Wrong

Hayes sends the group to a Red Zone. No choice: it's an order. A previous squad didn't come back. The Division wants to know why.

**The zone:** Underground laboratory beneath the Luxor. Phase 2 instability. Severe contamination (WIL save every 3 hours, d4 damage). 4-6 temporal echoes. A corridor where time flows backward.

**What they find:** Bodies of 2 agents from the previous squad. 1 surviving agent, hidden, traumatized, with 2 visible Quirks. The data, intact, in an armored briefcase. A class-B Oculus fragment (Hayes didn't mention this).

**The combat:** 4 temporal echoes (4 HP, 8 STR, 12 DEX, 6 WIL, temporal claw d6, WIL save on critical damage or accelerated aging). This is the hardest fight yet. Don't hold back.

> **To the Warden:** This is the scene where a PC probably dies. Don't force it, but don't protect them either. If a PC reaches 0 STR, they die. The game works this way. Death has weight because it's real.

### Scene 2: Replacement

If a PC dies, the player creates a new character (15 minutes). The new PC inherits the team's shared debt and starts at Recruit tier. Hayes is pragmatic: "We lost Agent [name]. Your new colleague is Agent [new name]. Briefing at 06:00."

### Scene 3: The Fragment Dilemma

Hayes asks for the data. She gets it. The Oculus fragment is not in any report. If PCs mention it, Hayes takes it without comment. If they don't mention it, they have an unregistered class-B fragment. Zhou would pay $5,000. The Division doesn't know it exists.

+1 Loyalty if they hand over everything. +2 Corruption if they keep the fragment.

## Session 4: Who Are You? (Weeks 8-10)

The final session is less action, more consequences.

### Scene 1: Zhou Collects

If anyone worked for Zhou before, she asks a second, bigger favor. The PC who said yes the first time is in a weak position: Zhou knows things. If nobody worked for Zhou, she raises the stakes to $3,000-$5,000. For a PC with $1,500 in debt and eviction imminent, that changes everything.

**Zhou's job (if they accept):** Sabotage a Division convoy. Don't kill anyone, just delay it. Zhou wants to reach an Oculus fragment first. Corruption: +3. If discovered: fired from the Division (game over for that PC, unless they work for Zhou full-time).

### Scene 2: The Division Demands

Hayes convenes the group. Monthly results are "below expectations." Translation: the Division spent more on them than they recovered. Hayes offers a final bonus mission: Red Zone, high risk, high reward ($600 bonus). If they succeed, the month breaks even. If they refuse, the Division "reevaluates their contract."

A veiled threat. Not of death: of termination. And a fired Division agent with Quirks and medicine dependency has nowhere to go.

### Scene 3: The Final Choice

Possible combinations:

- **Division mission + refuse Zhou:** Loyal, poor, contaminated, but employed
- **Division mission + work for Zhou:** Playing both sides. Enormous risk, enormous potential gain
- **Zhou only:** Betray the Division. High corruption, money, but no safety net
- **Neither:** Refuse both. Division fires them. Zhou ignores them. Alone in Vegas with Quirks and no medicine

There is no right ending. There is the ending the PCs choose and the consequences that follow.

## Final Accounting (Example)

For a PC who did all Division missions and refused Zhou:

| Week | Income | Expenses | Balance | Debt |
|------|--------|----------|---------|------|
| Start | - | - | - | -$500 |
| 1 | $1,090 | $900 | +$190 | -$310 |
| 2 | $800 | $1,000 | -$200 | -$510 |
| 3 | $950 | $900 | +$50 | -$460 |
| 4 | $800 | $950 | -$150 | -$610 |
| 5-6 | $1,350 | $1,800 | -$450 | -$1,060 |
| 7 | $800 | $900 | -$100 | -$1,160 |
| 8 | $1,400 | $900 | +$500 | -$660 |

After 8 weeks: debt -$660, 1-2 Quirks, Loyalty 7-8, Corruption 0. Loyal, poor, contaminated. Two months of doing everything right and you're still $660 in the hole. Zhou watches. Waits.

For a PC who accepted Zhou once: debt around -$100, 1 Quirk, Loyalty 6, Corruption 2. Better financially, but Loyalty and Corruption are close. One or two wrong choices and they're UNSTABLE.

> *This is the question the game asks every table: how much is your soul worth when rent is three weeks overdue?*
