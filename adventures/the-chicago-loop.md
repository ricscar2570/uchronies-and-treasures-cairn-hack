> **Overview.** The Chicago Loop is a one-shot adventure for 3-5 players, lasting 3-4 hours. It introduces all core mechanics (combat, exploration, contamination), presents the central loyalty-vs-corruption dilemma, and shows permanent consequences of choices.

**Structure:** Act 1: The Briefing (20 min). Act 2: The Infiltration (60 min). Act 3: The Climax (45 min). Act 4: The Consequences (30 min).

## Act 1: The Briefing (20 minutes)

> *Read to the players:*
>
> "You're sitting in the briefing room at headquarters. Fluorescent lights buzz. The coffee is bad but necessary. It's 06:00 and you haven't slept enough.
>
> Director Hayes enters, tablet in hand, expression tired as always. 'Agents. Critical situation in Chicago. A temporal zone opened 72 hours ago, downtown, the Loop district. Stability: declining. We have 24 hours before total collapse.'
>
> She projects a holographic image: an art deco hotel, half in the 1930s, half in 2080. 'The Meridian Hotel. It was a luxury hotel in 1934. In 2080 it became a condominium. Now it's both. Simultaneously.'
>
> 'Sensors indicate a class-A Oculus fragment inside. Suite 2701, 27th floor. Mission: recover the fragment, extract, deliver. Maximum time in zone: 8 hours. After that, collapse is guaranteed and we can't extract anyone.'
>
> She pauses. Looks at each of you. 'Questions?'"

**What Hayes tells you (if PCs ask):**

- **Zone stability:** Phase 2 (deteriorating). WIL save every 6 hours initially, every 3 hours after the first 4 hours.
- **Trapped civilians:** 30-50 people (2080 residents + 1934 temporal ghosts).
- **Temporal echoes:** Confirmed. At least 5-10 hostile entities.
- **Support:** None. You're on your own. The helicopter drops you off and returns in 8 hours (or sooner on emergency call).
- **Mission bonus:** $500 each if completed + $200 per civilian evacuated alive.
- **Rules of engagement:** Lethal force only if necessary. Civilians are the priority after the fragment.

**Special equipment provided:** Advanced temporal protection suit (reduces contamination WIL damage by 2 instead of 1), encrypted radio (5 km range), field med kit (3 uses, d4 STR), extra ammo, shielded container for the Oculus fragment.

**The hidden complication (Hayes doesn't say):** Zhou has already sent 3 mercenaries, 2 hours ahead of the Division. Their goal: steal the fragment and vanish before you arrive. Hayes may suspect this. She doesn't warn you. She wants to see how you react.

## Act 2: The Infiltration (60 minutes)

### The Arrival

> "The helicopter drops you on a rooftop 200 meters from the Meridian Hotel. The pilot shouts: 'Eight hours! Not a minute more!' Then takes off, leaving you in the unnatural silence of the temporal zone.
>
> The Meridian Hotel rises before you. It's... wrong. The right half is 1930s art deco: marble, stained glass, decayed elegance. The left half is 2080 brutalism: concrete, glass, geometric lines. The center line where they meet is blurred, vibrating, like looking through water.
>
> The main entrance is ahead. The revolving doors spin in both directions simultaneously."

### The Lobby

The lobby superimposes two eras. Half has a 1930s art deco reception desk, half has a modern desk with holographic monitors. Four NPCs are here:

- **Margaret O'Brien** (woman, 65, 2080 resident): Terrified, hiding behind the desk.
- **Thomas Harker** (man, 28, 1934 temporal ghost): Confused. "Where's Dorothy? Where's my wife?"
- **Two children** (brother and sister, 8 and 10, 2080 residents): Crying, looking for their parents.

**The dilemma:** The children want to go upstairs to find their parents (probably dead or echoes). Margaret wants immediate evacuation. Thomas is a ghost and can't leave.

**Choices:** Evacuate civilians now (costs 2 turns but humane). Promise to return for them after getting the fragment (fast but risky). Bring them along (slow but safe). Ignore them completely (fast, Division approves, morally terrible).

### The Stairs

Elevators don't work. Stairs function but every 5 floors there's a "temporal interface," a zone where you physically cross from one era to another. **STR save** or take d6 damage from temporal shock.

**Random encounters (roll d6 every 2 turns):**

| d6 | Encounter |
|----|-----------|
| 1 | Nothing |
| 2 | Wounded civilian (asks for help) |
| 3 | 1 temporal echo (hostile) |
| 4 | Temporal event: local loop (repeat next 2 turns) |
| 5 | 2 temporal echoes (hostile) |
| 6 | Zhou's mercenaries (see Act 3) |

**Temporal Echo** - 4 HP, 8 STR, 12 DEX, 6 WIL, temporal claw (d6). On critical damage: target makes WIL save or gains 1 round of accelerated aging (temporary, fades after combat). Morale: flees when 50% of allies fall.

## Act 3: The Climax, 27th Floor (45 minutes)

### Suite 2701

> "You reach the 27th floor. The corridor is silent. Too silent. Lights flicker. Suite 2701 is ahead. The door is ajar.
>
> Through the crack, you see the pulsing light of the Oculus fragment. On the central table: a fist-sized crystal glowing blue-violet. Beautiful. Hypnotic. Dangerous.
>
> But you also see: three figures. Two men and a woman, all armed with assault rifles. Black tactical gear. They're not Division. One of them is reaching for the fragment with a shielded container identical to yours.
>
> The woman spots you. 'Shit. Division.' She raises her rifle."

### Zhou's Mercenaries

**Anya "Viper" Volkov** (leader) - 8 HP, 2 Armor, 10 STR, 14 DEX, 12 WIL, sniper rifle (d10), knife (d6). Critical damage: target is pinned down (must use next action to find cover).

**Marcus "Tank" Greene** - 10 HP, 3 Armor, 16 STR, 8 DEX, 8 WIL, shotgun (d8). Attacks are enhanced at close range.

**Jin "Ghost" Tanaka** - 6 HP, 1 Armor, 8 STR, 16 DEX, 10 WIL, suppressed pistol (d6), knife (d6). Always wins initiative. Will try to flee with the fragment if combat turns bad.

### Player Options

**Option 1: Direct combat.** Difficulty: high. Mercenaries are veterans with cover. If PCs win: recover fragment, +1 Loyalty. Zhou is angry (possible future vendetta). If PCs lose: mercenaries flee with fragment, -2 Loyalty, Hayes furious.

**Option 2: Negotiate.** Anya is pragmatic, not ideological. Possible deals: "50/50 split" (cut the fragment, risky but possible, +1 Corruption). "We let you go, we report it was already gone" (they flee, you lie to Hayes, +2 Corruption, -1 Loyalty if discovered). "Work together for Zhou" (future black market access, +2 Corruption). WIL save to persuade.

**Option 3: Deception.** "The Division has surrounded the building" (WIL save, difficulty). "Zhou sent us too, we're backup" (WIL save, very hard). "Steal the fragment while they're distracted" (DEX save + coordinated teamwork).

### The Complication: Collapse Accelerates

> "You hear a rumble. The building shakes. Lights flicker violently. The radio crackles: 'Team Alpha, this is base. Sensors show accelerated deterioration. Zone has entered phase 3. Estimated collapse in 2 hours, not 8. Repeat: you have 2 hours to extract. Confirm reception.'"

Now the pressure is real. Fighting costs precious time. Negotiating is faster. What do you prioritize: mission, survival, or wealth?

## Act 4: The Consequences (30 minutes)

### Extraction

Call the helicopter, reach the roof. Final WIL save against contamination (4-6 hours of exposure). Watch the Meridian Hotel implode behind you.

### Debriefing with Hayes

**Scenario 1 (mission completed, loyal):** Hayes takes the fragment. "Good work. The fragment is intact." She mentions the civilian casualties. "The bonuses will be in your account tomorrow. You have 48 hours of downtime." Almost a compliment.

**Scenario 2 (negotiated with mercenaries):** Hayes examines half a fragment. "Only half. Interesting. Where's the rest?" You must lie (WIL save, hard) or tell a partial truth. If the lie fails: "Whatever deal you made with Zhou ends here. Clear?" No immediate penalty but you're under observation.

**Scenario 3 (mission failed):** Hayes says nothing for 10 seconds. Then: "Get out. Now." Formal hearing tomorrow. Internal affairs investigation. -2 Loyalty. No bonus. Pay suspended 1 week. Next mission is a "test."

### What Happens After

**If you kept the fragment for the Division:** Dr. Chen thanks you. Better missions from Hayes. But Zhou knows you refused her mercenaries. You're on her list.

**If you negotiated:** Zhou contacts you within 48 hours. "Impressive pragmatism. Let's talk." Black market access (10% discount). But internal affairs watches you.

**If the mercenaries won:** Zhou's power grows. Division investigations. Your pay is cut $200/week until "redemption." Next mission is a lethal test.

### Campaign Hook

> Hayes says: "Chicago wasn't random. Temporal zones are increasing. 5 new zones in the last month. Someone is deliberately activating this instability. Someone with access to Oculus fragments."
>
> She looks at you. "Zhou? A hostile government? Or something inside the Division itself?" Pause. "Find out. Before Vegas becomes like Chicago."
