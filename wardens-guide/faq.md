# Frequently Asked Questions

## Rules

**How does combat work without attack rolls?** You roll your weapon's damage die, subtract the target's Armor, and apply the remainder to their HP. Attacks always hit. The question isn't "do I hit?" but "how much damage do I take in return?"

**When should I call for a save?** Only when the outcome is uncertain AND the stakes are meaningful. If a PC with an Ex-Military background wants to field-strip a gun, no save needed. If they want to disarm a bomb while the building collapses, DEX save.

**Can PCs have more than 3 Armor?** No. 3 Armor is the maximum. Heavy tactical armor (3 Armor, bulky) is the best protection available.

**What happens when WIL reaches 0 from contamination?** The PC becomes a temporal echo. This is permanent. The player creates a new character.

**What happens when WIL reaches 0 from non-contamination sources?** The PC falls into catatonia (unresponsive). They can be healed with a week of medical care.

**How does the anti-rejection medicine work?** Without it, you are Deprived (can't recover HP or attributes) and your WIL degrades by 1 per week. It costs $250/week and takes 1 inventory slot. Missing it is a death sentence on a timer.

## Setting

**Is the Oculus event real history?** No. In the game's timeline, it happened in 2025. The current year is 2080. Everything before the Oculus is real-world history. Everything after is fiction.

**Can PCs leave Las Vegas?** Yes, but why? Their contract with the Division binds them financially. Their medicine dependency binds them medically. Their Quirks bind them socially (try explaining accelerated aging to an airport security guard). Vegas is a cage with open doors.

**Is Zhou evil?** No. Zhou is pragmatic. She exploits desperate people, but so does the Division. The difference is that Zhou is honest about the transaction. Hayes pretends it's patriotism. Neither is "good."

## Balance

**The economy seems impossible. Is this intentional?** Yes. The $100/week deficit at Recruit tier is the game's engine. Without it, there's no pressure toward corruption. If your players are comfortable financially, you're doing it wrong.

**How lethal should combat be?** Very. A pistol does d6 damage. Average starting HP is 3.5. One shot can drop a PC to 0 HP and trigger critical damage. Players should avoid combat. When they can't avoid it, they should stack every advantage.

**How fast do Quirks accumulate?** In a typical 10-session campaign, a PC who does mostly Yellow Zone missions will accumulate 1-2 Quirks. A PC who regularly enters Red Zones will get 3-4. Reaching 5 (echo threshold) should be rare but possible.

## Session Zero

**What should I cover?** Themes (violence, corruption, body horror, economic desperation, PC death). Safety tools (X-Card, Lines & Veils). Tone expectations (this is not a power fantasy; it's a survival horror game with spreadsheets). Character creation (do it together). First scene (read the Division briefing).

**How do I handle players who want to be "good guys"?** Let them try. The system doesn't punish goodness. It just makes it expensive. A PC who stays loyal and refuses Zhou will have more debt, more stress, and fewer resources. But they'll also have Hayes' trust, better missions, and their self-respect. Whether that's worth it is the whole point of the game.
