## Narrative Arcs (10-15 Sessions)

### Act I: Survival and Discovery (Sessions 1-5)

PCs are fresh recruits. They learn the systems: first missions in Yellow Zones, first paychecks, first deficits, first Zhou contact. The economy tightens slowly. The first Quirk appears. Players discover that honest play leads to debt.

**Warden focus:** Teach the mechanics through play. Show the economic spiral. Introduce Hayes as fair-but-cold, Zhou as friendly-but-dangerous. Don't rush the dilemmas.

### Act II: Corruption and Choices (Sessions 6-10)

Red Zone missions begin. The economy becomes critical. PCs have 1-3 Quirks. At least one PC has probably dealt with Zhou. Instability becomes possible. The first PC death is likely. The big questions emerge: who are you becoming?

**Warden focus:** Increase pressure on all fronts simultaneously. Money, contamination, loyalty, and corruption should all be in play every session. This is where the game's systems shine together.

### Act III: Consequences and Resolution (Sessions 11-15)

Red/Black Zone missions. The conspiracy emerges: someone is deliberately destabilizing temporal zones. PCs must commit to a faction. Multiple Quirks per character. Echo transformation is a real threat.

**Warden focus:** Bring all threads together. Every past choice has consequences now. Zhou calls in favors. Hayes demands results. The endgame approaches.

## Factions and Their Agendas

### The Temporal Division (Hayes)

**Wants:** Stabilize temporal zones, recover Oculus technology, maintain government control. **Methods:** Bureaucracy, hierarchy, expendable agents. **Weakness:** Underfunds its people, creating the very desperation Zhou exploits.

### Zhou's Black Market

**Wants:** Monopoly on temporal technology, access to Oculus files, profit. **Methods:** Bribery, patience, leverage, never direct violence (until necessary). **Weakness:** Depends on corrupted Division agents; if the Division cleans house, Zhou's network collapses.

### The Refugees (Emergent Faction)

**Wants:** Safety, medicine, a way out of Vegas. **Methods:** Community, mutual aid, occasional desperation. **They emerge** around session 5-8 when PCs discover that the people living in Yellow Zones aren't criminals; they're trapped.

### The Sentient Echoes (Mysterious Faction)

**Wants:** Unknown. Some seem to be trying to communicate. Some are hostile. Some are... organized? **They emerge** around session 10+ when PCs realize not all echoes are mindless. Something is happening in the deep zones.

### How Factions Interact

Zhou sells weapons to the Refugees (at a markup). The Division hunts Refugees in Yellow Zones (unauthorized residents). Sentient Echoes attack both sides equally. Hayes suspects Zhou is connected to the escalating instability but can't prove it. Everything is connected. Nothing is simple.

## Long-Term Consequences

### Tracking Choices

Keep a simple log: Session 3: "PCs accepted Zhou's first offer. Marco carried the package." Session 5: "PCs found unregistered fragment, kept it." Session 7: "Chen died. Replaced by Agent Reyes."

These notes become plot hooks. The package Marco carried? It contained plans for a Division raid. Zhou used the intel. People died. This comes back at session 10.

### Delayed Consequences (Examples)

- **Session 2 choice** (kept a small artifact): By session 8, Zhou mentions she knows about it. Leverage.
- **Session 4 choice** (helped civilians instead of completing objective): By session 6, those civilians help PCs in return. Or one of them was a Zhou spy.
- **Session 6 choice** (lied to Hayes about the fragment): By session 10, Internal Affairs investigation.

## Possible Campaign Endings

### Ending A: Loyalty Redeemed

PCs stayed loyal despite the cost. Debt is massive but Hayes promotes them. They uncover the conspiracy (a rogue Division scientist is deliberately creating temporal zones). The Division stabilizes Vegas. The PCs are heroes. But at what cost? Quirks, dead friends, permanent contamination. They won, but they're not the same people who started.

### Ending B: The Corruption Kingdom

PCs went all-in with Zhou. They helped her access the Oculus files. Zhou now controls temporal technology. The Division collapses. Vegas becomes Zhou's kingdom. PCs are rich, powerful, and damned. The contamination continues. The zones expand. But hey, the rent is paid.

### Ending C: The Precarious Balance

PCs played both sides. Neither Hayes nor Zhou fully trusts them. The conspiracy is partially uncovered. Vegas is neither saved nor doomed. The PCs survive, barely, in a grey zone. The most realistic ending. The most unsatisfying. The most human.

### Ending D: The Final Sacrifice

One or more PCs sacrifice themselves to destroy the source of instability (the original Oculus device under the Luxor). Vegas is saved. The zones begin stabilizing. The surviving PCs walk away from the Division, from Zhou, from everything. The dead are remembered. Sometimes heroes are just desperate people who ran out of options.

### Ending E: The Escape

PCs realize Vegas is doomed and leave. They flee the city, taking whatever money and artifacts they can carry. The Division marks them as deserters. Zhou marks them as assets who escaped. They start over somewhere else, with Quirks and nightmares. The zones continue expanding. Someone else's problem now.
